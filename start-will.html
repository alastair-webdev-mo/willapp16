Guardians

If you do not have any children under the age of 18 or any dependants, just select No and continue.

If you do have children under 18 or dependants, here you can write into your Will who you would like to be their legal guardians. This is done in a 2 step process.

Step 1 - Adding a Dependant

Fill in all the requested fields and click the "Add Dependant" button. Your dependant will then be added and appear in the table at the top of the page, along with 2 new buttons - Add a Dependant and Add a Guardian

You can add more dependants by clicking the "Add a Dependant" button

Step 2 - Adding a Guardian

Clicking on the "Add a Guardian" button opens a new form. In the first field within this form, choose your selected dependant from the drop down list, then choose a guardian from the second drop down list. Initially the only option in the Choose Guardian field will be Add New Person. Select this option and complete the resultant form. This person is then available within all your people drop down fields going forward.

The final stage is choosing whether this guardian is a Primary Guardian or a Substitute Guardian.

You can have multiple Primary guardians for each dependant, and these chosen people will have first option to become legal guardians if needed. Substitute guardians will only be contacted if the Primary guardians are unwilling or unable.

Gifts

Find Out More

Specific gifts are distributed before the rest of your estate and include:

• Property
• The right to live in a property
• Life insurance
• Cash gifts
• Specific items (Jewellery, cars, family heirlooms etc)

Specific gifts are paid out of the estate first, and should be described in a good amount of detail in the Will to avoid gift failure.

If a beneficiary dies before you then the specific gifts will be added to the remaining estate.

Adding Executors

Find Out More

An executor is the person or professional service appointed to carry out the wishes in your Will when you pass away. They are responsible for all aspects of administering your estate, including:

• Payment of any inheritance tax
• Distributing assets
• Valuing your estate
• Gathering all necessary financial and legal documents
• Locating all known bank accounts and savings accounts

This substantial amount of duties can last for months; therefore we recommend Damsons Estate Administration to do the job for you.

Why choose Damsons Estate Administration

Damsons Estate Administration provides a professional executor service that takes away all of the issues commonly associated with being executor to an estate.

Benefits of using Damsons Estate Administration include:

• All money received from the estate will be distributed immediately according to the instructions set out in your Will.
• Get the best price possible for assets and charges e.g. estate agent fees, stock broker fees.
• Immune to family pressures that are sometimes the cause of family feuds when estates are distributed.
• Your estate will have its own dedicated Estate Administration expert.
• With no hidden fees, Damsons Estate Administration charges a flat fee of 3% of your final estate value when you pass away.
• We will update your family regularly on any progress made.
